Current Threat Landscape

  • Pro-Russian hacktivist groups (Server Killers, Digital Revolt, Matryoshka 424) launched DDoS attacks on Kosovo government and media sites in 2024. (Balkan Insight; The Record)
  • Financial crime remains significant ‚Äî the 2020 Treasury theft is still the most costly cyber incident. (ILPS, DCAF)
  • Insider threats and weak IAM persist across public institutions.
  • DDoS and defacement attacks on media and state sites continue. (Committee to Protect Journalists, 2023)
  • Kosovo nationals implicated in international cybercrime prosecutions (e.g. BlackDB marketplace, 2025). (US DOJ, 2025)

Cyber Capacity and Governance

The Cyber Security Agency (CSA), created under Law No. 08/L-173 (2023), coordinates incident response and sectoral oversight.
The National Cybersecurity Strategy 2023‚Äì2027 defines responsibilities for operators and digital service providers.
The State Cybersecurity Training Center (2024) enhances national capacity with USAID and EU support.
Reports from QKSS (2024) and Kosovo 2.0 (2025) highlight progress but persistent capacity gaps.
The National Cyber Security Index gives Kosovo a score of 59.7/100 (2025).

Risk Outlook (2025‚Äì2026)

  • Continued DDoS activity aligned with geopolitical events.
  • Insider-enabled fraud and BEC-style scams remain probable.
  • Response times expected to improve as CSA structures mature.
  • Implementation gaps persist until full staffing and reporting enforcement. (QKSS 2024)

Recommendations

  1. Maintain DDoS protection and public communication procedures.
  2. Harden identity and payment workflows; enforce least privilege.
  3. Comply with CSA and sectoral reporting requirements.
  4. Enhance CERT transparency and information sharing.
  5. Invest in training and simulation exercises. (USAID CIDR 2024)
